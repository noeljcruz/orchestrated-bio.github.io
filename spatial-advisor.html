Spatial Advisor

Agentic Spatial Transcriptomics

An AI agent that guides your entire spatial transcriptomics analysis workflow. Spatial Advisor profiles your data, recommends preprocessing and analysis methods tailored to your tissue and platform, executes each step with best-practice parameters, and auto-adapts when results suggest a different analytical path. Supports cell-type annotation, cross-sample comparison, ligand-receptor analysis, and spatial statistics — all while keeping you in control.

Key Features

Automated Data Profiling

Automatically characterizes your dataset — identifies tissue type, spatial platform (Visium, MERFISH, CosMx), gene coverage, and quality metrics before recommending next steps.

Adaptive Method Selection

Recommends the right tools for each analysis step — normalization, clustering, spatial statistics, cell-type deconvolution — adapting recommendations as results come in.

Step-by-Step Execution

Executes each analysis step with optimized parameters, generates visualizations, and explains results in context — like having an experienced bioinformatician guiding your work.

Auto-Adaptive Workflow

If clustering reveals unexpected heterogeneity or QC flags issues, the agent automatically revises its approach and suggests alternative analyses — no manual debugging needed.

How It Works

1

Upload Your Data

Provide your spatial transcriptomics dataset (AnnData, h5ad, or count matrix). The agent profiles your data and identifies the platform, tissue type, and quality characteristics.

2

AI Plans Your Analysis

The agent proposes a tailored analysis pipeline — QC, normalization, feature selection, dimensionality reduction, spatial clustering, and downstream analyses — explaining each choice.

3

Execute & Adapt

Each step runs with optimized parameters. Results are visualized, interpreted, and used to inform the next step. If the data suggests a different approach, the agent pivots automatically.

Use Cases

Tumor Microenvironment

Map the spatial organization of immune cells, tumor cells, and stroma in cancer biopsies. Identify co-localization patterns that predict treatment response.

Neuroscience & Brain Mapping

Analyze spatial gene expression in brain tissue sections. Delineate anatomical regions, identify cell types, and discover spatially variable genes.

Drug Development

Evaluate drug effects on tissue architecture at single-cell spatial resolution. Compare treated vs. control samples to identify on-target and off-target spatial signatures.
